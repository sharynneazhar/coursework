27 ## What to Implement
28 #### [Allocation]
29 
30 > `void* buddy_alloc (int size);`
31 
32 On a memory request, the allocator returns the head of a free-list of the
33 matching size (i.e., smallest block that satisfies the request). If the
34 free-list of the matching block size is empty, then a larger block size will be
35 selected. The selected (large) block is then splitted into two smaller
36 blocks. Among the two blocks, left block will be used for allocation or be
37 further splitted while the right block will be added to the appropriate
38 free-list.
39 
40 #### [Free]
41 
42 > `void buddy_free(void *addr);`
43 
44 Whenever a block is freed, the allocator checks its buddy. If the buddy is free
45 as well, then the two buddies are combined to form a bigger block. This process
46 continues until one of the buddies is not free.
47 
48 How to find buddy and check if the buddy is free?
49 Suppose we have block B1 of order O, we can compute the buddy using the formula
50 below.
51 
52 > `B2 = B1 XOR (1 << O)`
53 We provide a convenient macro BUDDY_ADDR() for you.
